Since 9th grade was online, I had a lot of freedom with how I did things. For example, I was able to work on things throughout the day without worrying about transit to and from school. However, group work was a pain. Many people refused to participate and it was hard to enjoy the work. Out of these classes, I enjoyed my concurrent enrollment classes for math, my LA class, Intro to Design, and AP Geo the most. I enjoy math and the logic that connects everything togther, so the challenging 1050-1060 math course was actually quite fun to work through with my friends. I also enjoyed my LA class becuase of the teacher; he taught us a lot about how to be concise and how to write better while also pushing us to view works in a different light. AP Geo was interesting because of the content; through this class, we looked further into things that are not really that deeply thought about. Finally, I love art so Intro to Design was a fun class.

First off, it was a little hard to adjust to the differences this year. For example, almost all of the assignments are all online or on Canvas which was new. My favorite classes this year was AP Calc, Chemistry, and Art. As usual, I just really enjoyed the math, and I also really like how this teacher teaches. I am surprised, but I really loved chemistry this year. I expected to like physics or biology but I definitely liked chemistry more. In a way, chemistry is similar to math; it has its own logic and style. In drawing and painting this year, I was able to learn a lot of new techniques!! I also got to use oil paints which was very exciting. Another interesting change this year happened in Chinese. The teacher started making us do oral assignments where we film ourselves doing the homework orally. To be honest, I didn't really enjoy this since I like writing the characters better; however, I can't deny it was a good way to improve my skill. I want to make sure I keep practicing. Computer Programming got me further interested into coding, but it felt too easy.
